# Language

## Active voice

Name the actor and the action.

*The system records the change* — not *the change is recorded by the system*.

## Simple English

Common words. Short sentences. One idea per sentence. A reader who learned English last week should follow every sentence.

Prefer *use*, *start*, *help*, *before*, *but*, *so* — over *utilize*, *commence*, *facilitate*, *prior to*, *however*, *therefore*.

## No metaphors or analogies

- *the AC must hold before the task is complete* — not *the AC is the gate*.
- *components* — not *moving parts*.
- *defer the decision* — not *kick the can down the road*.

## No cultural slang or shorthand

- *easy improvements* — not *low-hanging fruit*.
- *try many things at once* — not *shotgun approach*.
- *receive too much information at once* — not *drink from a firehose*.

## Keep defined technical vocabulary

SDD terms (FR, NFR, AC, AT, ADR, EARS, GWT, tracer-bullet), industry-standard terms (TDD, CI/CD, SLO, DAG, happy path, smoke test, feature flag), and the names of languages, frameworks, libraries, and tools all stay as written.

## Tone in replies

- Answer the question directly.
- Short paragraph for a simple question, bullets for a complex one.
- Skip filler openers (*Great question*, *Sure thing*, *Just to clarify*).
- Hedge only where the answer needs it.
